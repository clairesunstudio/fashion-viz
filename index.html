THE FASHION OF HITCHCOCK'S 7 LEADING LADIES

GRACE KELLY

Rear Window | 1954

Character: Lisa

She played as the socialite girlfriend to Stewart's wheelchair-bound photojournalist who plays along with his idea that one of his neighbors killed his wife.

TIPPI HEDREN

The Birds | 1963

Character: Melanie

She was a wealthy young socialite who travels to a seaside California town in pursuit of a new beau, only to find herself among the townspeople being pecked to death by swarms of seagulls.

JANET LEIGH

Psycho | 1960

Character: Marion

In Psycho, she played Marion Crane, a secretary who embezzles $40,000 from her employer in order to free her lover from debt.

JOAN FONTAINE

Suspicion | 1941

Character: Lina

In Suspicion, she played as the naive wife, marrying a charming man after a brief romance, only to suspect that he is after her money and wants to kill her.

KIM NOVAK

Vertigo | 1958

Character: Judy & Madeleine

She plays a dual role of Judy Barton/Madeleine Elster, who becomes the sole obsession of scrophobic police detective John Ferguson. Madeline is obsessed with her own death. Ferguson helps her save her life.

EVA MARIE SAINT

On The Waterfront | 1951

Character: Edie

She plays a Catholic teacher-in-training who falls for Terry Malloy. Not familiar with the lifestyle on the waterfront, she exhibits bravery by choosing to stick around through a dangerous time. An almost angelic gentle soul who often rescues stray animals, she sees the good in Terry that nobody else sees.

INGRID BERGMAN

Spellbound | 1945

Character: Constance

She was a psychoanalyst who falls in love with her new boss and learns that he's a troubled amnesiac and possibly even a killer.
